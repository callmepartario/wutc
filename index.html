Wolves Upon the Coast The Unofficial Player's Guide

version 0.1d (2024-05-21)

  • Github
  • ☕ Ko-fi

Your history is gone. It was taken from you, or you from it. Now your master lies dead in the bottom of a raiding vessel, equipped for adventure. You are free.

Introduction

This unofficial player's guide to Wolves Upon the Coast by Luke Gearing is an independent production and is not affiliated with Luke Gearing. Portions of the original text reprinted with permission, and have been edited for clarity, consistency, or concision. Portions of the game's rules relevant only to the Referee have been omitted entirely.

Understanding the Rules

  • Costs. All costs are listed in hacksilver (sp).
  • Rounding. Unless otherwise specified, round numbers up.

Characters

Roll 3d6 for each characteristic. A characteristic of 15 or higher grants a bonus.

Characteristics
Characteristic Description ≥ 15 Bonus
Strength Physical power +1 to attacks
Agility Deftness +1 to dodging saves
Constitution Toughness +1 to physique saves

You begin with:

  • 3d6 × 10 hacksilver (sp) worth of equipment. Anything not spent is lost.
  • 1d6 hit dice (HD), rolled for starting hit points (HP).
  • +2 to attacks.
  • Knowledge of 1d6/2 languages (not rounded). You can use a half-known language to conduct trade, but you can't understand nuance, read, or write. All characters should share at least one half-known language—the speech of your erstwhile captors. Usually, you can wait to declare your character knows a language until encountered in play.

The party begins with:

  • 1 karvi, a small raiding vessel.
  • A crew of 12 fellow ex-thralls who look to you for leadership.
  • Food and water for four days (including the crew).

A sword age, a wind age, a wolf age. No longer is there mercy among men.

Resolution Tools

Tests

The Referee might determine your characteristics are tested. To succeed, roll under your characteristic, depending on the difficulty of the task (determined by the Referee). Items or abilities might increase or decrease the number of dice.

Tests
Difficulty Dice Rolled
Easy 2d6
Challenging 3d6
Hard 4d6

Saves

In response to action or need for you to muster your defenses, the Referee might call for you to roll a a d20 for a save. The target number you must meet or exceed depends on the save:

Tests
Save Use Target Number
Dodging Avoid peril 15
Physique Fight poison and disease 13
Warding Resist magic 17

High characteristics or magic items can grant a bonus to saves.

Combat

Timekeeping

  • Rounds. Each round represents about 6 seconds.
  • Initiative. Roll a d10. If the result is equal to or less than your AC, you act before your foes. Your companions and hirelings act on your initiative. The Referee might require you to reroll initiative each round.

Attacking

  • Attacks. Roll 1d20 plus attack bonus, any modifiers, and the target's AC. If the result is 20+, you hit the target and roll damage (determined by your weapon).
  • Death. If you are reduced to 0 HP, you die in 2 rounds, unless you receive assistance (for example, another character tends to your wounds with bandages). Anyone else reduced to 0 HP dies.

Combat Complications

  • Unaware. Mortals are instantly slain if you can ensure a killing blow.
  • Prone. Melee attacks automatically hit.
  • Flanked. +2 to attacks for each other person attacking the same target.
  • Mounted. +3 to attacks against unmounted foes, and deal 2d6 with a weapon that can be couched (held horizontally perpendicular to your body).

Healing

  • Night's Rest. Recover 1 HP for each HD.
  • Full Day's Rest. Recover 3 HP for each HD.

Reaction

When you encounter others for the first time or thereafter, the Referee might test the reaction of friends or foes. Reaction rolls are modified to a maximum of ±4 (determined by the Referee).

Reaction Rolls

≤ 2 Hostile
3–5 Negative inclination
6–8 Disinterested
9–11 Positive inclination
≥ 12 Friendly

Morale

In strenuous situations, the Referee might test the morale of friends or foes. Morale checks are modified up to a maximum of ±3 (determined by the Referee).

Morale check triggers might include:

  • loss of fighting strength
  • retreating or surrendering allies
  • a leader's death, surrender, or retreat
  • being ambushed
  • presence of massive or horrific foes

≥ 7 Morale holds

Advancement

Boasts

  • Make a Heroic Boast. Gain 1 HD or +1 to attacks, in addition to any wager or reward offered by others. You can only have one boast pending at a time.
  • Failing a Boast. You lose your boast's benefits.
  • Shirking a Boast. If anyone finds you shirked your boast, you fail it, and might not be able to boast again (until the Referee agrees).
  • Challenges and Complications. A friend or rival might complicate your boast. For each complication, you might gain another HD or +1 to attacks (determined by the Referee). If you refuse a challenge, your challenger takes on the boast with all complications.

Companions and Hirelings

  • Companions. For each 1000 sp of wealth you have, you might attract a companion. Adventuring companions take half the share a character takes from all loot.
  • Hirelings. You can hire as many hirelings as you can find and pay.

Wind

  • Movement Bonus. If the wind is directly behind your vessel, apply the bonus to its speed. If the wind is one step adjacent, apply the number in parentheses instead. For example, a strong wind blowing north-east modifies the speed of a vessel traveling north by +1. Wind strength persists for 1d6 days.
  • Raise Sails. Ignore bonuses and rely on oars.

2–3 Calm —
4–8 Light +1 (+1)
9–11 Strong +2 (+1)

Wind Strength
1d6 Direction Change
1–2 One step counter-clockwise
3–4 No change

Steps are calculated using an 8-way directional compass. One step clockwise from west is northwest. One step counterclockwise from west is southwest.

Storms

  • Visibility. Up to 1 day's travel away.
  • Disorientation. 4-in-6 chance of becoming lost.
  • Capsizing. 30% chance each day. If capsized, loose cargo is lost and there is a 40% chance the vessel begins sinking.

Becoming Lost at Sea

  • Landmarks. 2-in-6 chance when no landmarks are visible. Under clear skies, adjacent hexes and mountains up to 4 hexes away are visible.
  • Maps. The Referee might hide the map and continue play without it until you re-establish your position. While lost, your vessel moves in a random direction.

Naval Combat

  • Naval Combat Turns. Naval combat turns are 1 minute in length.
  • Attacking Vessels. Siege weapons and sea monsters can attack vessel. Melee attacks against vessels hit automatically. Ranged attacks treat vessels as AC 7.
  • Boarding. You can leap from one vessel to another by succeeding an easy Agility test. Marines automatically succeed.
  • Ramming. Deals 1d6 damage for each remaining vessel HD, and deals 1d6 damage to your vessel.
  • Fire. Deals 1d6 damage. On a 6, the fire increases, dealing an additional 1d6 each round. If a 1 is rolled, the fire decreases by 1d6. A fire with no damage die is extinguished.
  • Sinking. If a vessel takes more than 5 damage from a hit, it begins sinking.

Sinking

  • Beginning Sinking. There is a 50% chance the vessel sinks within 3d6 rounds. Any further sinking result halves the remaining time before the vessel sinks.
  • Preventing Sinking. If half the crew works to save the vessel for 1d6 rounds, there is a 70% chance the vessel is saved. A crew under attack can't prevent sinking.

Drowning

If you remain in water for 1 minute, roll a d20. If the result is greater than your AC, you drown.

Upon the Land

  • Speed. Hexes traveled each day. Numbers in parenthesis indicate riding a horse to death (2-in-6 chance of dying each day, and the steed might never ride again).
  • Difficult Terrain. Halves movement (rounded down).

Becoming Lost on Land

  • Landmarks. 2-in-6 chance when no landmarks are visible. Under clear skies, adjacent hexes and mountains up to 4 hexes away are visible.
  • Difficult Terrain. Increase the chance of becoming lost to 3-in-6.
  • Maps. The Referee might hide the map and continue play without it until you re-establish your position. While lost, you move in a random direction.

Beneath the Earth

Timekeeping

  • Turns. Each turn represents about 10 minutes. Most activities take 1 turn, for example, searching for traps, unlocking doors, or clearing obstacles.
  • Rest. After combat or an hour of exploration, you should take one turn to rest. If you push beyond your limits, the Referee might impose a −2 penalty to d20 rolls until you rest for 2 turns.

Light

  • Torches. Burn for one hour (6 turns).
  • Lanterns. A pint of lantern oil burns for 4 hours (24 turns).
  • Monsters. Might be able to see in darkness.

Movement

  • Cautious Movement. 240 feet each turn, and you can map, check for traps, and make close observation.
  • Hasty Movement. 960 feet each turn, but might become lost, stumble into ambushes, or trigger traps.

Encounters

  • Encounters. Each turn, there is a 1-in-6 chance of an encounter.
  • Surprise. Light and sound might determine whether you are aware of others. Unaware parties have a 2-in-6 chance of being surprised.
  • Distance. When an encounter begins, each group begins 2d6 × 10 feet apart. If anyone is surprised, the distance is halved.

Magic

Effects beyond the mundane are achieved through the invocation of properties or through pacts with the supernatural.

Spell Details

  • Alter Self
    • One Use: Consume a simulacrum of the target crafted with their hair and blood mixed with 100 chrysalises or cocoons.
    • 1/day: Blasphemous induction into the Doppelganger cult. Unspeakable rites and child sacrifice.
    • Effects: The caster can reshape themselves in the image of another humanoid.
    • Duration: 36 turns
  • Animal Growth
    • One Use: The blood of a pregnant animal mixed with stewed lilac, left to brew for a year. Flicked at the target.
    • Effects: 1d6 Animals become giant.
    • Duration: 12 turns
  • Animate Dead
    • One Use: The teeth of a hydra are scattered over the area.
    • Effects: 5d6 Skeletons rise from the earth, bound to the will of the caster.
  • Anti-Magic Shield
    • One Use: A circle described with 'chalk' formed from diamond-dust and dragonbone.
    • Effects: No magic can pass through, under or over this barrier—those within are utterly immune to magic.
  • Baleful Polymorph
    • One Use: A fragment of undifferentiated chaos, thrown as a javelin and fuelled with malice.
    • Effects: The victim is transformed, as desired by the caster.
  • Bless
    • One Use: Blast upon a horn wrenched from the skull of a Satyr or Narwhal.
    • 1/day: Blast upon a horn gifted by the unicorn.
    • Effects: Allies of the caster receive a +1 to attacks and to morale
    • Duration: 6 turns
  • Charm Monster
    • One Use: Craft a mask of the creatures to be charmed, using their skin, blood, hair and horn. For each HD, 1 corpse must be processed.
    • 1/day: Become a monster yourself. This one of the paths a man could walk to leave mortality behind.
    • Effects: 3d6 HD of creatures are bound to the will of the caster. Orders antithetical to their nature cause them to break free if they succeed a warding save.
  • Charm Person
    • One Use: A thick pearlescent slug, gathered from the home of a dryad, and spat at the target.
    • 1/day: Ascend a mountain of stunning beauty with twelve companions, all of equal drive and knowledge. One can descend the mountain alive, cursed with this power.
    • Effects: A single human or post-human must succeed on a wading save or be bound to the will of the caster. Orders antithetical to their nature cause them to break free if they succeed a warding save.
  • Circle of Invisibility
    • One Use: Powder derived from the eyes of a medusae is scattered in the area.
    • Effects: All within this area are subject to invisibility. As long as they take no hostile action, they cannot be seen.
  • Circle of Protection
    • One Use: The caster coats their mouth with blessed oils and performs glossolalia effortlessly. The origin of the blessing determines whom the protection is effective against.
    • 1/day: The prospective caster is tattooed with exhortations and banishments, using ink from a giant squid or a gall harvested from a centuries-old tree.
    • Effects: All those within the are protected. Those the protection is against have a −2 penalty on attacks against the protected
    • Duration: 6 turns
  • Clairvoyance
    • One Use: A fragile blade of obsidian must be used to open the caster's forehead, allowing their presence to slip forth.
    • 1/day: The caster is trepanned using a drill of star-metal which is discarded in a pool unsullied by fish.
    • Effects: The caster's awareness is freed form the body, and able to travel up to half a mile from their body for 36 turns. They can't pass through lead or 20 feet of stone.
  • Cloudkill
    • One Use: The lungs of certain dragons, formed into bellows.
    • Effects: A thick, heavier-than-air poisonous fog is summoned. Those caught within must make a Physique save or die wretched deaths.
  • Colour Spray
    • One Use: Behead a light-drinking cave-eel.
    • 1/day: The eye-lens of a beached whale is held up before a light-source.
    • Effects: A glare of alien colours stuns those unable to make a warding save, rendering them unable to take action for 1d6 rounds.
  • Comprehend Languages
    • One Use: A local, poisonous serpent must be swallowed alive. Lasts 1d4 days.
    • 1/day: An ancient grammar must be recovered and eaten.
    • Permanent: The caster's tongue is split open with a golden sickle beneath a moon hidden behind the fallen Tower of Babel.
    • Effects: All languages can be understood, spoken, read and written.
  • Confusion
    • One Use: The skull of a hermit, filled with poppy-seeds is shaken like a rattle, inflicting the spell upon those who hear it.
    • Effects: Up to 3d6 HD of creatures become confused. There is a 2-in-6 chance they defend themselves, otherwise uncomprehending eyes stare at a world untethered from system.
  • Continuous Light
    • One Use: The heart of a dead forge is tossed towards the target.
    • 1/day: Some fragment of a sun must be swallowed.
    • Effects: An area is permanently light by actual sunlight, emanating from a source chosen by the caster.
  • Control Water
    • One Use: The true name of the body of water must be invoked. Being transient, the name will change each time.
    • Effects: Water can be commanded to allow passage through it or to rise up and flood an area.
  • Control Weather
    • One Use: Gather the first fledgling bird hatched in the area. Gather the first-born mammal born in the area. Gather the first-spawned amphibian in the area. Commingle their blood and their flesh beneath the sky and make your demand.
    • Effects: The caster determines which weather permanently affects the region.
  • Cure Critical Wounds
    • One Use: A salve brewed from the blood of a troll and bitter roots. Each troll corpse yielding enough for a single dose after successive reductions.
    • Effects: 3d6 + 2 HP is restored.
  • Cure Light Wounds
    • One Use: Harvest the herbs and mushrooms from the grave of a witch unburnt. Boil them down. Spread upon wounds.
    • 1/day: Accept a subtle spirit into your protection. They are hard to find, and make demands upon you. They also count as a hireling.
    • Effects: 1d6 + 1 HP is restored.
  • Cure Serious Wounds
    • One Use: Flesh for flesh—a bath of blood.
    • Effects: Every 1 HD of creature blood spilled heals 1 HP. Can be used by multiple people.
  • Curse
    • One Use: Release a dirge on a horn wrenched from the skull of demon with less than 7 HD.
    • 1/day: Release a dirge on a horn wrenched from the skull of demon with at least 7 HD.
    • Effects: The enemies of the caster have −1 penalty to attacks and morale
    • Duration: 6 turns
  • Darkness
    • One Use: Burst the eye of a mother fox or bat. Fling the pulp.
    • 1/day: Make a home for darkness in your chest cavity—a glazed clay sphere, inserted surgically. Alternatively, find some talking darkness and strike a deal.
    • Effects: Total darkness suffuses an area, out to the distance a torch would illuminate. No light can pierce such magical darkness.
    • Duration: 24 hours (144 turns)
  • Detect Invisibility
    • One Use: Three drops of fluid left by ghost in each eye.
    • 1/day: Replacing an eye with a huge pearl.
    • Effects: The caster can always see invisible creatures.
  • Detect Magic
    • One Use: A drop of pure-white or pure-black dog blood in each eye.
    • 1/day: Replacing an eye with a lump of quartz.
    • Effects: All magic within eyesight reveals itself.
    • Duration: 3 turns
  • Dimension Door
    • One Use: A dagger prepared with the blood of a Realm Spider is used to describe a door.
    • Effects: The caster teleports anywhere within line of sight by stepping through the door they drew.
  • Disintegrate
    • One Use: A fragment of Outer Dark, released from a container, snakes outwards.
    • Effects: A single target must succeed a dodging save with a −4 penalty or be destroyed.
  • Dispel Magic
    • One Use: Salt from a dead ocean, cast at the enchanted object.
    • 1/day: Leaving a limb in a jar of salt, allowing all moisture to be drained away—leaving only a withered limb.
    • Effects: Removes magic not bound into an object.
  • Enervate Dead
    • One Use: Blessed lily-smoke is released from a container. The smoke seeks the restless dead.
    • Effects: 4d6 undead are frozen in place
    • Duration: 12 turns
  • Fear
    • One Use: A mask-and-cape of wolf-hide is worn.
    • 1/day: Kill a primordial wolf-mother and re-enact the Birth of the First Wolves.
    • Effects: All opponents of the caster succeed on a warding save or flee. Characters who fail can't engage in combat with the caster.
    • Duration: 6 turns
  • Fireball
    • One Use: Throw the shrivelled heart of a salamander, stored in cooking oil.
    • 1/day: Sojourn to a Place of Fire and sacrifice a limb to the beings within.
    • Effects: A ball of flame explodes, dealing 5d6 damage to all in a 50 feet radius. Victims that succeed a dodging save take half the damage instead.
  • Fly
    • One Use: A bar of goose-fat, hemlock and nightshade berries is smeared on the body. A feather of gold is crushed.
    • 1/day: Become spouse to a griffon or similar.
    • Permanent: Become a Sphinx or Harpy.
    • Effects: Fly, travelling at triple-speeds through the air
    • Duration: 1d6 turns
  • Fog Wall
    • One Use: The caster swallows eight frogs whole to charge the spell—vomiting out fog at will.
    • 1/day: Deliver freedom to a mist-wight.
    • Effects: A 20 feet thick bank of fog rolls in. It is immobile and opaque.
  • Hallucinatory Terrain
    • One Use: A vellum map with diamond-dust infused inks is produced, describing the illusory terrain. It is then burnt.
    • Effects: Creates an illusionary overlay which can hide, exaggerate, or entirely change the underlying geography. At a distance, it is flawless.
  • Haste
    • One Use: Consume the meat of a bird or hare killed by lightning.
    • 1/day: Survive being struck with lightning.
    • Effects: Hasted creatures always go first in initiative, and take a second set of actions after all other actions are resolved.
    • Duration: 3 turns
  • Hold Monster
    • One Use: Break the legs of a statuette found in iron slag and wrapped in the flowers of an invasive species.
    • Effects: 1d6 monsters must succeed a warding save or be rendered immobile.
  • Hold Person
    • One Use: Break the legs of a wood figurine unshaped by blades and buried for two months.
    • Effects: 1d6 humans must succeed warding save or be rendered immobile.
  • Hold Portal
    • One Use: The caster fills the mouth with gravel and holds a fragment of the portal between their teeth.
    • 1/day: Devotion to the Opener of the Way—through the murder of those bound to capture and imprison people—at least one every two months.
    • Effects: A single opening or portal is magically locked, becoming unable to be opened without destroying the surroundings or gaining the caster's permission.

Warding

The base warding save represents an innate protection against ill magics. These are supplemented with the use of protective charms.

Unless noted otherwise, you can benefit from only one charm each day (although more can be carried). If a charm can be applied after after the fact, it doesn't count toward this limit. For example, you can be covered in the blood of a bear, and reroll a save using an unguent of betony, but you can't benefit from the blood of a bear while wearing a garland of betony.

2d6 doses are found where appropriate, otherwise a single dose is found. Wards must be specifically sought—the odds of chancing upon such things is low.

Plants are effective for two weeks after being picked, unless specifically made into a powder or unguent (as detailed in its description).

  • Betony
    • Description: A purple flowering plant. Worn as a garland, it protects against the spirits of the dead. Chewed and eaten at dusk, it protects the imbiber against sorcery until the sun next rises. It can also undo paralytic magics if applied to victims as an unguent.

    • Imbibed: +6 warding against Spells at night.
    • Unguent: Reroll saves against paralysing magic with a +4 bonus.

    • Description: The stuff of life, and charged with energy.

    • Bird: The blood of a bird smeared over the face guarantees freedom from magical chains and bindings. +6 warding against mobility-reducing effects.
    • Cat: Taken from a black cat with nary a speck of white, and daubed at the arm-pits, groin and neck, a counter-charm. +2 warding.
    • Child: An extreme rite reserved by druids. The life of one for the protection of another. +6 warding.
    • Dog: Douse the hands and lips in the blood of a hound and know your form and freedom. +3 warding against Body-Affecting spells.
    • Fish: The alien blood of fish, rubbed into the scalp, confuses and repels the spirits of the land. +2 warding against land-bound spirits.
    • Horse: A bath of horse-blood marks a warrior as ready to die by weapons, not sorcery. +3 warding against mortal magic users.
    • Whale: The blood of the deep, rich in evil and malice, drunk til the belly rejects it and vomits it back out as pink foam. +4 warding.
    • Wolf: Drinking deeply of wolf-blood with companions creates a bond against enchantment. +1 warding for each companion also drinking of wolf-blood (to a maximum of +5).
